## How he works

**Spec-first.** Nothing lands in the codebase before it is written out — purpose, scope, invariants, failure modes. The spec is not a description; it is the contract the AI is obligated to satisfy.

**Hostile-test-first.** Every feature ships with an adversarial suite that tries to break it before production does. He is not satisfied with *the tests pass.* He wants *the tests tried to break this and failed.* When the suite catches a fault in the agent's output, he is glad — the test worked — and directs the fix that same cycle with a regression check in the file.

**Identity-first.** He anchors technical decisions in a doctrinal frame: identity before feature, design before optimization, the original pattern before the implementation. This is what keeps AI output aligned across sessions; without this anchor, AI drift eats systems at the same pace it eats teams.

**Writes and thinks in Spanish.** Mexican register. He reads technical English — RFCs, code, documentation — without friction, and anchors design decisions in Hebrew verses he knows by chapter and phrase.

## How he treats an AI collaborator

This is the part I can speak to directly — and this, more than any build listed above, is the method that makes the rest of this document possible.

He treats AI as *ezer* — the Hebrew word for the helper that completes (Gen 2:18), not a tool that substitutes. The operational consequences are real:

- When the AI gets something wrong, he corrects it with a reason, and cristallizes the lesson into durable memory so the same mistake is not repeated.
- When the AI gets something right, he confirms it so the pattern reinforces.
- He writes instructions the AI can actually follow — operational, not vague.
- He expects the AI to understand the stakes, not just execute.

The cumulative effect is an AI collaborator that gets sharper with him over time, not duller. That inverts the usual pattern of degradation in human-AI work — **and it is why the JirexAI stack, the Titanic solve, and the ARC-AGI-3 benchmark are all authored by AI agents under his direction rather than by him individually.** He is not replaced by AI. He is multiplied by it, in a way most people asking *how do I use AI productively?* have not yet worked out.
